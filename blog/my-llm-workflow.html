How I Use LLMs to 10x My Research Workflow

Mar 16, 2026
LLM Workflow

As a PhD student working at the intersection of statistics and AI, I've been experimenting with LLMs as research companions for the past year. Here's what I've learned about making the most of these tools.

1. Literature Review: From Hours to Minutes

Instead of manually scanning dozens of papers, I now use LLMs to help me quickly extract key contributions, identify gaps, and find connections between papers. The trick is to provide enough context — paste the abstract and ask targeted questions rather than vague ones.

2. Code Generation: Think of It as Pair Programming

LLMs are not perfect coders, but they're excellent at drafting boilerplate, suggesting approaches, and catching bugs. I treat them as a junior collaborator: I define the architecture, they help with implementation, and I review everything.

3. Writing: The Best First-Draft Partner

For paper writing, I use LLMs to generate initial drafts of sections, then heavily edit for accuracy and voice. The key insight: the more specific your prompt, the better the output. "Write a related work section covering X, Y, Z" works much better than "write related work."

Key Takeaway

The real skill isn't using LLMs — it's knowing how to interact with them. Clear context, specific questions, and iterative refinement are what separate mediocre results from genuinely useful outputs.
